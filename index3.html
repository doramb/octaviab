About Octavia E. Butler

Octavia Estelle Butler - born on June 22, 1947, lived until February 24, 2006 - was an American science-fiction author and a multiple recipient of the Hugo and Nebula awards. In 1995, Butler became the first science-fiction writer to receive a MacArthur Fellowship. By then, her books had been translated into 10 languages, selling more than 1 million copies altogether. In the years since, her fanbase has only grown.
